# Job-fit handoff for certification-gap research

**Purpose:** Durable summary of recent `/job-fit` evaluations so another agent can research **certification programs, coursework, and portfolio-building paths** that address the **trainable** gaps in target roles.

**Important framing:** Not every weakness in these job descriptions is a training gap. Some are **structural fit mismatches** around ambiguity, travel, consulting load, and high-pressure forward-deployed ownership. Another agent should distinguish:

- **Trainable gaps** — reasonable to address with certifications, guided coursework, labs, or focused portfolio projects.
- **Structural mismatches** — poor fit signals that are **not** solved by collecting credentials.

## Recurring structural mismatches

These are **not** primarily certification problems.

### 1. High-ambiguity forward-deployed environments

Many weak-fit roles expect:

- weak or evolving playbooks
- strong self-direction
- multiple stakeholder groups
- discovery-to-deployment ownership
- fast decisions under pressure

This maps directly onto Tyler's documented risk profile around follow-through, scoping, executive function, confidence, and ambiguous ownership. See [`docs/career-fit-context.md`](../career-fit-context.md).

### 2. Heavy travel / event load / consulting load

Repeated in OpenAI, Google, Anthropic evangelism, and likely in some advisory roles.

This is a **sustainability** question, not just a skill gap.

**Nuance (2026-05-20):** Tyler reports strong **customer focus and brand representation** from **Apple retail and B2B business team** experience (see [`certification-recommendations-2026-05-20.md`](certification-recommendations-2026-05-20.md) — section on customer focus vs evangelist JDs). That is **not the same** as AI-vendor evangelism (stage demos, ecosystem pressure, heavy travel) or **technical solution crafting** under ambiguity. Agents should not treat “evangelist role = skip” as “no customer-facing strengths”; calibrate separately for **KPMG-style guided client work** vs **evangelist SKIPs**.

### 3. Deep domain specialization

Example: Anthropic Life Sciences.

This is not “get a quick certificate and you are done.” It is a domain-credibility gap that may require a longer-term strategic decision, not a tactical credential.

### 4. Senior architect / deployment-owner expectations

Some roles expect not just skills, but a **proven identity**:

- trusted advisor
- pre-sales architect
- seasoned enterprise operator
- field lead under pressure

That is partly narrative, partly experience accumulation, not just coursework.

> Which certifications or programs best support the kinds of roles that are **actually good bets** for Tyler, rather than simply trying to brute-force him into the least compatible job descriptions?
