Use these as **starting points**, not as the only sources. Always verify dates and links.

## Research & science

- [arXiv cs.AI / cs.LG](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent) — preprints (note: not peer-reviewed until published elsewhere)
- [Nature AI / ML](https://www.nature.com/subjects/machine-learning) — peer-reviewed
- Major lab blogs: MIT News, Stanford HAI, etc.

## Agentic / developer ecosystem

- GitHub blog, MCP/A2A spec repos, LangChain/CrewAI release pages when materially new

## Reputable aggregators (secondary — link to primary)

- The Verge AI, Ars Technica AI, Reuters technology, official company posts surfaced via search

## Ranking heuristics

1. New **standards**, **regulation**, or **frontier model** access changes  
2. Shipped **consumer** or **enterprise** products with clear user impact  
3. **Peer-reviewed** or replicated science with external validation  
4. Credible **open-weight** or **benchmark** shifts  
5. Deprioritize: opinion-only posts, engagement bait, unsourced leaks
