Across domains and frontier models, authors **Patrik Wolf** et al. report widespread consistency violations and a **macro fallacy**: fine-grained subpopulation prompts often yield better population estimates than direct population prompts, implying models hold granular knowledge they fail to propagate upward. They propose **statistical self-consistency** as a reference-free evaluation criterion. | [arXiv:2607.15277 — Statistical Self-Consistency](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15277v1) |

The pipeline identified molecules **MP20** and **A1** with potency comparable to ceftriaxone monotherapy while avoiding over-mined scaffolds and common nitro antimicrobial motifs — a concrete AI-for-drug-discovery win against rising resistance. | [Nature Biomedical Engineering — Outracing antibiotic resistance](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41551-026-01761-0) |
| 10 | Generative AI | Scientific Reports publishes lightweight generative-AI drug-discovery assistant for 4GB GPUs | A **Scientific Reports** paper published **July 18** describes an end-to-end generative assistant for early-stage small-molecule discovery that runs on consumer hardware such as an **NVIDIA GTX 1650** (**4GB VRAM**), claiming roughly **93%** infrastructure-cost reduction versus enterprise setups. The pipeline integrates a **SELFIES**-based **LSTM** generator, **XGBoost** toxicity screening across **12** assays, hybrid property prediction, and **ESMFold** structure calls, with CPU fallback when GPU memory is insufficient. Authors report stable validation loss and **0.790** weighted AUC on toxicity classification after threshold tuning. | [Scientific Reports — Lightweight generative AI drug discovery](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-026-61237-8) |
