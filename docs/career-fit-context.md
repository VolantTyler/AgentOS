## Situation (high level)

Tyler is examining **long-standing dissatisfaction and instability** in work (without detailing specific departures in this public doc). Ongoing struggles cluster around:

- **Memory and executive function** (organization, follow-through, completion)
- **Consistency** of delivery over time
- **Ambition and sustained drive** (especially under ambiguous conditions)
- **Confidence and self-worth** in professional settings

**Central fear (his words, paraphrased):**  
*Is there a job or environment that actually fits me — or am I not cut out for “work success” in the conventional sense?*

Tyler has a **formal ADHD diagnosis** (he is comfortable stating that in this repo). Challenges overlap strongly with **executive-function-heavy** job demands. A useful framing from reflection (including with Gemini) is **mismatch between ADHD-shaped executive function and typical job structures**, not “lack of ability.”

## Core reframe agents should use

1. **Not a lack of ability or value** — evidence from career and projects supports real capability (see [`TECH_STACK.md`](TECH_STACK.md)).  
2. **Fit and structure matter** — many conventional roles overweight skills that are **harder for this profile** (open-ended self-management, heavy context switching, ambiguous ownership).  
3. **Blunt but fair:** Tyler is **not** suited to *every* environment; he is **especially** suited to environments that **play to front-end/UI strengths** and provide **clarity, structure, and accountable support**.  
4. **Psychological layer:** low self-esteem and self-doubt can **amplify** performance gaps; there is a tendency to read struggle as **personal failure** rather than **structural mismatch**. Naming that pattern helps without toxic positivity.

## Career fit profile — environments

### Seek

- Strong **product + design** collaboration  
- **Clear scope**, defined ownership, explicit priorities  
- **Async-friendly but structured** communication norms (templates, checklists, expected response shapes)  
- Managers who **guide, prioritize, and reduce ambiguity**  
- Room to lean into **front-end, UI, design systems, accessibility**, or **documentation / DX**  

### Avoid (unless compensated with heavy support)

- **High ambiguity** with little managerial scaffolding  
- Roles that assume **constant self-direction** and heavy **multitasking** without guardrails  
- **Deep backend / systems ownership** as the primary expectation (unless Tyler explicitly chooses growth there with support)  
- Cultures that punish **“wrong”** more than they support **learning in the open**

## How agents can support execution (concrete)

- **Externalize executive function:** PR checklists, task templates, explicit “definition of done,” smaller visible chunks.  
- **Communication templates** for async updates (what changed, what’s blocked, what decision is needed).  
- **Scoping discipline:** force **S / M / L** estimates, explicit **non-goals**, and **assumption lists** before build.  
- **Confidence as a skill:** encourage opinions **with evidence**, separate **incorrect** from **incapable**, rehearse articulating tradeoffs (aligns with [`identity-brief.md`](identity-brief.md)).  
- **Job search / networking:** translate this doc into **filters** and **questions for hiring managers** (not as a confession — as **fit diligence**).
