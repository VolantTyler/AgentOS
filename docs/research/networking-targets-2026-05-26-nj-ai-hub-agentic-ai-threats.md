## Nonprofits securing AI — challenges to discuss

Pressures her work implicitly targets; useful for coalition / support questions:

1. **No security bench** — rarely a CISO or AI red team; one “tech person” wears many hats
2. **HIPAA + AI overlap** — vendors, BAAs, PHI in prompts/logs/RAG; consumer AI tiers often unfit
3. **Safety ≠ classic security** — crisis language and escalation are not covered by a normal pen test
4. **Ambiguity gap** — gray-zone messages break keyword guardrails
5. **Wrapper risk** — low-cost bots inherit foundation-model policy holes
6. **Evidence gap** — boards ask “is it safe?” but teams lack test logs and control maps
7. **Budget** — enterprise GRC out of reach; **open standards** are the scalable path

## What is OWASP? (reference)

**OWASP** = **Open Worldwide Application Security Project** — a **nonprofit, vendor-neutral, global community** that publishes **free, open guidance** to improve **software and AI security** ([owasp.org/about](https://owasp.org/about/)).

- Classic work: **OWASP Top 10** (web application risks)
- AI work: **[OWASP GenAI Security Project](https://genai.owasp.org/)** — e.g. **Top 10 for LLM applications**, **Top 10 for Agentic AI applications**, **red teaming guides**, **AISVS** (testable requirements)
- **For tonight:** OWASP is **shared vocabulary** between builders and security teams — not a regulator or a certification by itself
